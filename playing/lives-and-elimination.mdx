Your hearts — also called lives — are what keep you in the tournament. You start each tournament with a fixed number of hearts, and you lose one each time you fail or miss a round. Lose all of them and you're eliminated.

## How many hearts you start with

The host sets the number of starting hearts before the tournament begins. Most tournaments give each player **2 hearts**. You can see your current hearts at all times in the status banner at the top of `/play`.

Filled hearts (`❤️`) are lives you still have. Empty hearts (`🤍`) are lives you've already lost.

## When you lose a heart

You lose 1 heart in either of these situations:

- **You fail a round** — you submit your answers but your score falls below the round's pass threshold (default 60%).
- **You miss a round** — the round closes and you never submitted. The system records a score of 0% on your behalf, which counts as a failure.

<Note>
  Practice rounds **never** cause you to lose a heart, regardless of your score.
</Note>

## When you're eliminated

You're eliminated when you've lost all of your hearts. As soon as your heart count reaches the limit, your account is marked as eliminated and you can no longer submit answers for tournament rounds.

The status banner on `/play` changes from your hearts display to "Out of the game" when you're eliminated.

## What happens after elimination

Being eliminated doesn't lock you out of the site. You can still:

- Visit `/play` to see the current and past rounds.
- View the player list at `/players` to follow everyone still in the game.
- Play any open practice rounds — they're always available to everyone.

<Tip>
  Eliminated players can still cheer others on! Head to the `/players` page on the site to see who's still standing.
</Tip>

## Host adjustments

The host can restore players or adjust heart counts at any time. If you believe your hearts were changed in error, reach out to the host directly — they have the ability to bring eliminated players back into the game or correct a mistake.
