Practice rounds let you play a quiz without any risk to your tournament standing. They work exactly like regular rounds — you answer questions and get a score — but your result is just for you. No hearts are at stake and nothing you do in a practice round changes the tournament leaderboard.

## What makes practice rounds different

| | Tournament rounds | Practice rounds |
|---|---|---|
| Affects standings | Yes | No |
| Can lose a heart | Yes | No |
| Can be eliminated | Yes | No |
| Available after elimination | No | Yes |
| Always open | No | Yes |

## How to find practice rounds

Practice rounds appear in the **Practice rounds** section on your `/play` dashboard, below the main round card. Each listed round shows its title and, if you've already completed it, your score as a percentage.

<Note>
  If no practice rounds are listed, the host hasn't published any yet. Check back later.
</Note>

## How to play a practice round

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open /play">
    Scroll down past the current round card to find the **Practice rounds** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Try it">
    Click the **Try it** button next to the round you want to play. This opens the quiz.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Answer the questions">
    Work through each question at your own pace. Practice rounds have the same question types as tournament rounds: multiple-choice and true/false.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and see your results">
    Submit your answers when you're done. Your score is shown immediately as a percentage, along with a full review of which answers were correct.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reviewing a completed practice round

After you submit, you can come back and review your answers at any time. On the `/play` dashboard, the **Try it** button changes to **review** once you've submitted, and your score appears next to the round title.

<Tip>
  If you've been eliminated from the main tournament, practice rounds are a great way to stay engaged and keep playing while others compete.
</Tip>
