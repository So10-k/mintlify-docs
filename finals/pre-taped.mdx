The Grand Final was originally planned as a **live Saturday broadcast** with all four finalists on camera at once and the whole tournament list tuning in over a Zoho webinar. **That broadcast is cancelled.** The Grand Final is now being **pre-recorded** and sent out as a watch-anytime video link.

<Note>
  Sam (the host) sent every enrolled player an apology email titled **"Sorry — the Mia's Quiz Tournament finals are going pre-taped 💌"** the day before the original broadcast. If you didn't get it, check spam or [email Sam](mailto:appdev7710@gmail.com).
</Note>

## The change in plain English

| Was | Now |
|---|---|
| Live broadcast on Sat May 16 at 12:00 PM Eastern | Pre-recorded, sent out by email |
| Zoho webinar with everyone watching simultaneously | Watch-anytime video link you click whenever |
| All four finalists on camera at once | Each match recorded one-on-one with Sam |
| Real-time chat in the Zoho webinar | Post-show banter in the Discourse forum |
| One shot — if your wifi died, you missed it | Pause, rewind, watch in your pyjamas |

The **format itself didn't change**. Same three matches (losers' final → winners' final → championship), same questions, same scoreboard, same special effects, same trophy ceremony — just no live audience and no Saturday-at-noon time pressure.

## Why pre-tape instead of going live?

The short version is "Sam left it too late." The long version:

- **Tech complexity for a kids' show.** A live broadcast means juggling Zoho Webinar, the on-air `/watch` scene, the per-finalist `/live` answer UI, AV gear, and four finalists each on their own wifi at the same moment. Each piece works on its own; getting all four perfect at the same instant is a different problem.
- **Time-zone pain.** Some of the audience is across multiple time zones. A Saturday-noon-Eastern slot was awkward for at least a third of the list — a pre-taped video they watch on their own time is just kinder.
- **Better edit.** Recording each match separately means Sam can cut around stutters, retry effects that misfired, drop in proper interstitials, and generally make the video feel like a TV show instead of a livestream.

If the trade-off was "live and rough" vs. "pre-taped and polished," polished won.

## What didn't change

- **The questions.** Famous-places trivia for both bracket finals (separate sets), misc-hard for the championship. The championship set stays hidden from Sam since he might be playing.
- **The four finalists.** Karen, Marc, Grandpa, Sam — same matchups.
- **The bracket impact.** The pre-taped matches resolve the tournament for real. The eventual champion is Season 1's Mia's Quiz Tournament champion.
- **The forum.** [Tournament Talk](https://discuss.miaswebsites.art/c/tournament-talk) on the Discourse forum stays open for hot takes, predictions, and post-show banter. After the video drops, expect the spoiler-free discussion to migrate to a finals recap thread.

## Recording the matches one at a time

Each match — losers' final, winners' final, championship — is recorded as its own video. Sam runs the same `/live` quiz UI for the finalist(s) and `/watch` scene for the on-camera backdrop that would have been screen-shared into the live webinar. The questions, timer, effects, and scoreboard all behave exactly the way they would have on Saturday. The recordings are then edited together into one continuous show.
