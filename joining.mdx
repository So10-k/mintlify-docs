Signing up for Mia's Quiz Tournament takes less than a minute at **/join**. You give a display name and an email address, click **Send me a magic link**, and a one-time link arrives in your inbox. Click the link and you're enrolled — no password to create, no second form, no account to confirm.

<Note>
  /join is the **new-player door**. It uses a one-time **magic link** sent through Mia's own email pipeline (Resend or Brevo, whichever the host has selected). Returning players can sign in here too, but **/signin** is the faster door for you — it uses the MiaAuth code flow instead. Either page gets you to the same account.

## What you'll see

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to /join">
    Open **/join** in your browser. You'll see a card with two fields:

    - **Your name** — what other players (and Mia) will see on the standings and bracket. Up to 80 characters.
    - **Your email** — where the magic link lands. This is the address that identifies your account forever, so use one you actually read.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Send me a magic link'">
    The button creates your account in the tournament database (with the name you just typed) and asks Mia's email provider to send a one-time **magic link** to the address you provided.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Land on /join/check-inbox">
    The browser flips to a **Check your inbox!** confirmation page. The magic-link email is on its way — usually under a minute.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the email and click 'Play now →'">
    The email has the subject **"Your magic link"** and a single big button. Click it on the same device. Auth.js verifies the link, signs you in, and bounces you to **/play**.

    <Warning>
      Magic links are **one-time use** and expire after **24 hours**. If yours has expired or you've already clicked it, head back to /signin and ask for a new one.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  No password is ever asked for, set, or stored. The tournament has no password field anywhere.
</Note>

## What happens behind the scenes

When you submit the /join form, the tournament does three things in order:

1. **Validates your name and email** (1–80 chars for name, valid email address up to 254 chars).
2. **Creates your `users` row** in the tournament database with your chosen display name. If the host's `AUTHOR_EMAIL` matches yours, you're promoted to **author** role at this step.
3. **Calls Auth.js's email provider** to send the magic link. Auth.js stores a hashed token; the link itself contains the un-hashed half.

When you click the link, Auth.js verifies the token, marks your email as verified, and creates a database-backed session that keeps you signed in across visits.

## "Sign-ups are closed for now"

If the host has paused registrations, the form is hidden and you'll see this message instead:

> Sign-ups are closed for now. The first round drops soon!

When sign-ups reopen, the form comes back automatically — no action needed on your part. You'll also see this message if the host **closed registration after the tournament started**; in that case, only players who joined earlier can still sign in. Watch the home page for an announcement when sign-ups reopen.

## Switching browsers or devices

Open the magic-link email **on the same device** you used to sign up. Some mail apps pre-fetch links from a different IP, which can invalidate the token before you get to click it. If your link doesn't work on first click:

1. Go back to **/join** (or /signin) and request a new link.
2. Open the new email on the device you're actually going to play on.
3. Click the **Play now →** button — not a preview or "Open link" menu item.

## Already have an account?

You can use /join to sign back in too — the form accepts existing emails and just sends them a new magic link. But the faster path for returning players is **/signin**, which uses the MiaAuth code flow instead of email links. See the [Sign In guide](/signing-in) for details.

<Tip>
  /join is the only entry point that lets you pick a display name. If you ever want to change yours, ask Mia — name changes happen on the host side, not yours.
</Tip>
