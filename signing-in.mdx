If you've already joined the tournament, you don't need to sign up again. Head to **/signin** and tap **Sign in with MiaAuth**. MiaAuth — the tournament's email-only sign-in flow, powered by Auth0 by Okta — sends a one-time code to your inbox. Enter the code and you're back in with your account, hearts, and scores intact.

<Note>
  MiaAuth is the everyday sign-in method. There are no passwords, no social logins, and no SSO buttons. If you're a returning player, this is the page you want.
</Note>

## The two-screen sign-in

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to /signin">
    Open **/signin** in your browser. You'll see one big button labeled **Sign in with MiaAuth**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Sign in with MiaAuth'">
    The button hands you to Auth0's hosted page. Type the same email address you used when you joined, then ask Auth0 to send you a code.

    <Warning>
      Use the **same email** you signed up with. MiaAuth identifies you by email — a different address opens a new account, not your existing one.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox for a code">
    The email arrives within a few seconds. Open it, copy the **6-digit code**, and paste it into the Auth0 prompt. The code expires after a short window; if it times out, just ask for a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You land back on /play">
    Once Auth0 accepts the code, you're redirected straight to **/play**, signed in. Your hearts, scores, and standing all come back with you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  No password is needed at any point. Every sign-in uses a fresh MiaAuth code sent to your email.
</Note>

## What you'll see on the sign-in card

The /signin card shows three reassurance badges and one line of footer text:

- 🛡️ **Bank-grade** — Auth0 is the same identity platform used by Fortune 500 companies, banks, and healthcare systems.
- 🔑 **No passwords** — There's no password field. There never was.
- ⚡️ **10s sign-in** — Average round-trip on a typical inbox is about ten seconds.

The footer reminds you: **Sign-in is powered by Auth0 by Okta** — your email never touches Mia's servers without MFA-grade protection.

## "Registration's closed" — what it means

If MiaAuth bounces a brand-new email with a friendly **"Registration's closed for now"** banner, that's the tournament's sign-up gate, not a MiaAuth error. Existing players sail through; new emails are blocked until the host opens registration. If you're sure you have an account, double-check the email address — a typo will look like a new account to MiaAuth.

## Can't find your code email?

Check your spam or junk folder first. If nothing arrives after a minute or two:

1. Go back to **/signin** and request another code. Each request invalidates older ones.
2. Make sure the address is typed exactly right (one stray character is the most common cause).
3. Add the sender domain to your address book so future codes skip the spam filter.

<Tip>
  You can also use **/join** to sign back in. /join is mostly aimed at new players, but it accepts returning players too — same account, same hearts, just a slightly longer form.
</Tip>

## Legacy magic-link fallback (break-glass only)

If Auth0 is having an outage and you absolutely need to sign in, append **`?legacy=1`** to the /signin URL. A second form appears below the MiaAuth button: enter your email and you'll get a one-time **magic link** (subject: **"Your magic link"**) sent through Mia's own email pipeline. Click the link in the email and you're signed in.

<Warning>
  The legacy magic-link flow is intentionally not advertised on the sign-in page. It works, but you only need it when Auth0 itself is unreachable. Treat it as a break-glass option.
</Warning>

| Sign-in page | `/signin` |
| Primary method | **MiaAuth** — Auth0 passwordless email (one-time code) |
