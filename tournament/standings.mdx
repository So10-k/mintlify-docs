The standings page shows every enrolled player's rank in the current tournament. Scores are updated as each quiz round closes, so you can check back after every round to see where you sit.

## What the standings show

Each row on the leaderboard displays:

- **Player name** — your display name or email if no name is set
- **Cumulative score** — your total points earned across every quiz round you've submitted
- **Lives remaining** — the number of hearts you still have (shown as ❤️)
- **OUT badge** — displayed if you have been eliminated from the tournament

The top three players receive medal icons (gold, silver, bronze) next to their rank.

## How ranking works

Players are sorted by the following rules, in order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Highest cumulative score first">
    Your score is the sum of all round attempts you've submitted. The player with the most total points sits at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fewest strikes as a tiebreaker">
    If two players have the same score, the player who has received fewer strikes ranks higher.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Earliest registration as a final tiebreaker">
    If score and strikes are still equal, whoever signed up for the tournament first ranks higher.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What "OUT" means

When a player uses all of their lives, they are eliminated. Their row stays visible on the standings so you can see where they finished, but it appears dimmed and carries the **OUT** badge instead of a heart count.

<Note>
  Being marked OUT does not necessarily mean you stop playing. If the tournament has a losers bracket, eliminated players from Round 1 may get a second chance there. Check the [bracket page](/tournament/bracket) for details.
</Note>

## When the tournament ends

Once the host closes the tournament, the standings switch to **Final Results** and a champion card appears at the top of the page. The champion's name is displayed prominently with a crown, celebrating the winner for all players to see.

## Page availability

The host can temporarily hide the standings for all players (for example, to reveal results live). When the page is hidden you will see a "paused" notice instead of the leaderboard. The standings return automatically once the host makes them visible again.

<Info>
  If you see the paused notice, the host has temporarily locked the page. Check back shortly or watch for an announcement.
</Info>
