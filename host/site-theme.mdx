title: "Choosing a Visual Theme: Picture-book or Arcade Mode"
description: "Choose between the Picture-book and Arcade visual themes for your public bracket, players, and standings pages — changes go live within about 30 seconds."
---

Mia's Quiz Tournament has two visual themes for its public-facing pages. You can switch between them at any time from the host panel, and the change rolls out to visitors within about 30 seconds.

## The two themes

### Picture-book (default)

The original look for the site — bright sky-blue backgrounds, warm sun yellows, chunky navy borders, and the Fredoka/Quicksand typeface pairing. It is the default and is on by default when you first set up the tournament.

### Arcade (neon)

A dark mode inspired by the Supercell store aesthetic. It features dark backgrounds, neon glow effects, rarity-style badges, and a holographic title treatment. This theme swaps the sunny palette for a high-contrast, game-night feel.

## Which pages are affected

The theme setting applies only to the three public pages:

- `/bracket` — the tournament bracket
- `/players` — the players list
- `/standings` — the standings table

The host panel (`/host`), the play page (`/play`), sign-in, and all other pages are not affected by this setting.

<Note>
  The theme does not change the email styling. Announcement and MiaAuth (Auth0) sign-in emails always use the picture-book stationery regardless of which theme is active.
</Note>

## How to switch themes

1. Go to the host panel at `/host`.
2. Scroll to the **Site theme** section.
3. Click **Picture-book (default)** or **Arcade (neon)** — the currently active theme button is highlighted.
4. The change saves immediately. Public pages pick it up within approximately 30 seconds as the cache expires.

The current theme is shown below the buttons: either "Picture-book" or "Arcade (neon)".

<Warning>
  There is a cache delay of up to 30 seconds before the change is visible to players on the public pages. If you need to confirm the change has taken effect, wait a moment and then reload `/bracket`, `/players`, or `/standings`.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  **Picture-book** suits a casual, family-friendly or school tournament where you want a warm, approachable look. **Arcade** works well if your players are gamers or if you want a more dramatic, high-stakes atmosphere — for example, a late-stage playoff or a night-time live event.
</Tip>
