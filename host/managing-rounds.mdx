title: "Creating and Managing Quiz Rounds in Your Tournament"
description: "How to write, start, close, and reopen tournament rounds — including practice rounds, pass thresholds, and the full round lifecycle from draft to closed."
---

Rounds are the quizzes that players compete in. Every round you write starts as a **draft** that only you can see. You decide when to release it by pressing **Start Round N**, and you decide when to close it by pressing **Close Round N**. Only one real round can be active at a time.

## Round lifecycle

```
draft  →  active  →  closed
```

| Status | Visible to players | Accepts submissions | Can be deleted | Can be reopened |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `draft` | No | No | Yes | — |
| `active` | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| `closed` | Yes (results shown) | No | No | Yes |

<Note>
Practice rounds skip this lifecycle — they go straight to `active` when you save them and stay open until you delete them.
</Note>

## Writing a round

The **Write a new round** section at the bottom of `/host` contains the round editor form. Fill in all required fields and press **Save round** to create a draft.

### Round fields

| Field | Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Practice round** toggle | No | When checked, the round is always open, never awards strikes, and does not feed the bracket. |
| **Round title** | Yes | Up to 120 characters. Shown to players when the round is active. |
| **A little intro** | No | Up to 800 characters of prose shown above the questions. Use it to set the scene or give context. |
| **Pass score** | Yes | A percentage slider, default 60%. Players who score below this threshold lose a heart. |
| **Closes at** | No | An optional date/time after which submissions are no longer accepted. If left blank, the round stays open until you press **Close Round N**. |

### Questions

Each round needs at least one question. Every question has:

- A **prompt** (up to 400 characters)
- Between 2 and 6 **answer options** (up to 200 characters each)
- Exactly one option marked as correct — tap the circle next to the answer to mark it

<Tip>
You can add up to 6 answer options per question and remove extras down to a minimum of 2. Questions can also be saved to the question library for reuse across future rounds.
</Tip>

## Create and publish a round

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the round editor">
    Scroll to the **Write a new round** section at the bottom of `/host`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the round type">
    Leave the **Practice round** toggle unchecked for a real tournament round. Check it only if you want an unscored warm-up.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the round title and optional intro">
    Give the round a clear title (for example, "The Animal Round"). Optionally write a short intro paragraph that players see before the questions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the pass score">
    Drag the **Pass score** slider to the percentage players must reach to keep their heart. The default is 60%.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add questions">
    For each question, type the prompt, fill in at least two answer options, and click the circle next to the correct answer to mark it green. Press **+ Add another question** to add more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the round">
    Press **Save round**. The round is saved as a draft and appears in the **Rounds** list with a `draft` badge. Players cannot see it yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the round when ready">
    When you are ready to open the round to players, press **Start Round N** in the **Tournament** section. The round moves to `active`, players can now submit answers, and the tournament status changes to `in_progress` if it was still in registration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the round">
    Press **Close Round N** to end submissions. Scores are recorded, strikes are applied to players who failed or did not submit, eliminations are processed, and the bracket auto-advances winners by score.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pass threshold and what it means

The pass score you set determines who loses a heart when a round closes:

- A player who scores **at or above** the threshold passes. No heart lost.
- A player who scores **below** the threshold fails and loses one heart (gains one strike).
- A player who **did not submit** before the round closed is automatically given a score of 0 and loses a heart.

Once a player's strike count reaches the tournament's strike limit, they are marked **OUT**.

<Info>
The strike limit is set at the tournament level (default: 2 strikes = out). You can adjust individual players' heart counts manually from the **Players** section.
</Info>

## Practice rounds

Practice rounds are warm-up quizzes that are always open to players. To create one, check the **Practice round** toggle in the round editor before saving.

Key differences from real rounds:

- Go straight to `active` on save — no need to press Start.
- Never award strikes or cause eliminations.
- Do not feed the bracket.
- Eliminated players can take practice rounds.
- You can delete a practice round draft; active practice rounds can also be deleted from the **Practice rounds** list.

## Managing existing rounds

From the **Rounds** list on `/host`:

- **Delete** — available for `draft` rounds only. Permanently removes the round and its questions. This cannot be undone.
- **Reopen** — available for `closed` rounds. Sets the round back to `active` so players can submit again. You must close any currently active round before reopening a different one.

<Warning>
Reopening a closed round does not reverse any strikes or eliminations that were applied when it was first closed. Use the heart controls in the **Players** section to manually correct player standing if needed.
</Warning>
