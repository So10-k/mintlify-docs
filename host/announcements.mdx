The host panel gives you two ways to keep players informed: a bulk email tool for announcements and a countdown card that shows a live ticking clock on the home and play pages. Both are available from the main host panel at `/host`.

## Sending an announcement

The **Send an announcement** section is at the bottom of the host panel. It uses an audience selector so you can target the right people without maintaining separate lists.

### Audience options

The dropdown offers the following audience modes:

| Mode | Who receives it |
|---|---|
| Players still in the tournament | Everyone whose enrollment is not yet eliminated |
| Players eliminated (any round) | Everyone who has been knocked out |
| All signed-up players | Every enrolled player regardless of status |
| Eliminated in a specific round | Players eliminated in the round you select |
| Players who passed a specific round | Players who submitted a passing attempt in the chosen round |
| Enrolled but didn't submit a specific round | Players who were enrolled but never submitted for that round |
| Players with an exact strike count | Players whose recorded strike count matches the number you enter |
| Hand-picked players | A checklist of individual enrolled players |
| Every user (all accounts) | All registered accounts, not just enrolled tournament players — use with care |

For round-scoped modes, a second dropdown appears so you can select the specific round. For "Hand-picked players", a searchable checklist of enrolled players appears, with buttons to pre-select subsets (still in, eliminated, all).

A live recipient count updates as you make changes. The first five email addresses (masked) are shown so you can sanity-check who matches before sending.

### Merge variables

You can personalise any field — subject line, message body, or template fields — using merge variables. Each recipient gets their own values substituted at send time.

| Token | Substitutes to |
|---|---|
| `{name}` | Player's full display name, or email handle if no name is set |
| `{firstName}` | First word of the display name |
| `{email}` | Their email address |

Example: `Hey {firstName}, round 2 is live!` becomes `Hey Marc, round 2 is live!` for Marc.

### Email format

The **Format** dropdown lets you choose between plain text and a set of pre-built templates:

- **Plain text (default)** — Your typed message, sent with the standard sky-card picture-book stationery. URLs in the message are automatically linkified.
- **Schedule shift · public re-send** — Apology and corrected schedule card.
- **Bracket update · with live snapshot** — Your message plus a live bracket image that refreshes when recipients open the email.
- **Still in · congrats with bracket** — Personal congrats note for a player who survived the round, with a live bracket image.
- **Eliminated · gentle reveal** — Round recap that gently breaks elimination news near the end.
- **Time is running out · live countdown** — A live-updating countdown clock rendered at open time, with your message and a play button.
- **Tiebreaker · 5 questions, winner advances** — Sent to two players who tied, with a link to a tiebreaker quiz.

When you select a template, its fields appear for you to fill in. A **Show preview** button renders the email in an iframe so you can see exactly what recipients will receive.

All emails are sent from **Mia's Quiz Tournament** using the same picture-book styling as the MiaAuth (Auth0) sign-in emails.

### How to send an announcement

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick an audience">
    Open the Audience dropdown and select the group you want to reach. If the mode requires a round, select it in the secondary dropdown. Check the recipient count shown below the selector.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a format">
    Leave **Plain text** selected to write a free-form message, or pick a template from the Format dropdown. Templates show their fields and a preview option.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your subject and message">
    Enter a subject line (up to 140 characters). For plain text, write your message in the Message box. For templates, fill in the template fields. Use `{firstName}`, `{name}`, or `{email}` anywhere to personalise per recipient.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and send">
    Click **Review & send**. A confirmation card appears showing the subject, recipient count, and audience label. Click **Yes, send it** to dispatch. A green result banner confirms how many emails were sent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The "Every user" audience targets every registered account, not just enrolled tournament players. Double-check the recipient count before sending when using this mode.
</Warning>

## The countdown card

The **Countdown card** section (above the announcements section on the host panel) lets you put a live ticking clock at the top of the home page (`/`) and the play page (`/play`) for all signed-in visitors.

The card has three fields:

- **Label** — A short phrase shown above the digits, such as "Round 2 starts in". Up to 80 characters.
- **Target (your local time)** — A date and time picker. The countdown runs down to this moment.
- **Visible to players** — Checkbox. Tick to show the card; untick to hide it without losing the label and target values.

Click **Save countdown** to apply. The current state (showing or hidden, and the target datetime) is shown below the form.

<Note>
  The countdown card is only visible to signed-in users. Signed-out visitors do not see it.
</Note>

## Email provider

Announcements and MiaAuth (Auth0) sign-in emails both go through the same provider — either **Resend** or **Brevo**. You can switch between them from the **Email provider** section on the host panel if you are approaching one provider's sending limits. The switch takes effect within approximately 30 seconds.

To review delivery results and open rates, go to **Emails** (`/host/email-analytics`) from the host panel navigation links.
